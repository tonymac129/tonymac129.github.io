Ribosomes: Keyboard and Mouse

If you compare cell organells to different components of a laptop according to their functions, a cell's ribosomes would be the keyboard and the mouse of the laptop. The main function of ribosomes is that they produce proteins that determine what the cell does according to the DNA (blueprint) from the cell nucleus. mRNA (messenger RNA) transcribes the blueprint stored in nucleus, carries it to the ribosomes, and ribosomes will make amino acid chains according to the blueprint. The amino acid chains are proteins that act as commands that tell the cell what to do. Keyboard and mouse do the same thing, too. They produce commands that tell the laptop what to do by recieving the user input and convert them into code that the CPU can process and respond to the input. So in conclusion, ribosomes, keyboard, and mouse are similar because they all make commands that tell their parent element what to do.
